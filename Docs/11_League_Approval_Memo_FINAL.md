## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Sports Media, Inc. is seeking League approval to offer the Sports Media Charter charter procurement platform as an approved vendor solution for member teams. This memo outlines the program structure, safety controls, compliance framework, and risk mitigation measures.

**Key Points:**

- Sports Media Charter is a technology platform, NOT an air carrier
- All flights operated by FAA Part 135 certificated carriers only
- Participation is 100% voluntary for teams
- Does NOT affect team charter (roster travel)
- Comprehensive safety vetting and insurance requirements
- Full audit trail and reporting capabilities

---

## PROGRAM OVERVIEW

### What Sports Media Charter Is

Sports Media Charter is a mobile and web platform that enables teams to:

- Submit charter requests for executive, family, and sponsor travel
- Receive quotes from pre-vetted charter brokers
- Compare options and book flights
- Manage approvals and documentation
- Track flights in real-time

### What Sports Media Charter Is NOT

- NOT an air carrier or aircraft operator
- NOT a replacement for team charter (roster travel)
- NOT mandatory for any team
- NOT affiliated with League travel policies or CBA obligations

### Target Use Cases

| Use Case         | Examples                           |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Executive Travel | GM, President, ownership trips     |
| Front Office     | Scouts, operations staff           |
| Player Movement  | Trade deadline travel, free agency |
| Family Travel    | Approved family transportation     |
| Sponsor/VIP      | Partner hospitality travel         |
| Emergency        | Medical, security situations       |

## SAFETY AND COMPLIANCE CONTROLS

### Pre-Booking Verification

Before any flight is arranged, the following are verified:

**Operator Verification Checklist:**

- [ ] Part 135 certificate active and valid
- [ ] Certificate not suspended, revoked, or under emergency order
- [ ] ARGUS or Wyvern safety rating confirmed
- [ ] Insurance certificate current and limits adequate
- [ ] No pending FAA enforcement actions
- [ ] Crew currency and qualifications (upon request)

### Safety Rating Requirements

| Rating Organization | Minimum Acceptable | Preferred |
| ------------------- | ------------------ | --------- |
| ARGUS               | Gold               | Platinum  |
| Wyvern              | Registered         | Wingman   |

**What These Ratings Mean:**

- **ARGUS Gold/Platinum:** Operator has passed rigorous safety audit, maintains documented safety management systems, no outstanding safety issues
- **Wyvern Registered/Wingman:** Operator has completed comprehensive on-site safety audit and meets industry best practices

### Insurance Requirements

| Coverage                       | Minimum Limit   | Purpose                          |
| ------------------------------ | --------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Aircraft Liability (10+ seats) | $50,000,000 CSL | Passenger/third-party protection |
| Aircraft Liability (<10 seats) | $25,000,000 CSL | Passenger/third-party protection |
| Broker E&O                     | $2,000,000      | Booking errors protection        |
| Broker Non-Owned Aircraft      | $5,000,000      | Additional liability layer       |
| Platform Provider Liability    | $2,000,000      | Platform errors protection       |

### Documentation Requirements

For each booking, the following documentation is available:

| Document              | Timing          | Retention |
| --------------------- | --------------- | --------- |
| Operator Certificate  | Before booking  | 7 years   |
| Insurance Certificate | Within 4 hours  | 7 years   |
| ARGUS/Wyvern Report   | Upon request    | 7 years   |
| Passenger Manifest    | Before flight   | 7 years   |
| Booking Confirmation  | At booking      | 7 years   |
| Post-Flight Invoice   | Within 48 hours | 7 years   |

## INCIDENT REPORTING

### Reporting Commitments

| Incident Type           | Reporting Timeline | Recipient     |
| ----------------------- | ------------------ | ------------- |
| Safety incident (any)   | Within 24 hours    | League + Team |
| Accident/injury         | Immediately        | League + Team |
| Regulatory violation    | Within 24 hours    | League        |

### Incident Categories

| Category        | Definition                            | Response                                   |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| **Critical**    | Accident, injury, safety of life      | Immediate notification, full investigation |
| **Significant** | Regulatory issue, stranded passengers | 24-hour notification, written report       |
| **Moderate**    | Major delay, service failure          | 48-hour notification, corrective action    |
| **Minor**       | Inconvenience, minor issues           | Tracked internally, reported in aggregate  |

### Investigation Process

1. Immediate containment and response
2. Notification to affected parties
3. Root cause investigation (completed within 72 hours)
4. Corrective action plan development
5. Written report to League (within 7 days)
6. Follow-up verification of corrective actions

## APPENDIX A: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### For League Operations

**Q: Does this affect our team charter program?**
A: No. Sports Media Charter covers only executive/auxiliary travel, not team charter operations.

**Q: Is this mandatory for teams?**
A: No. Participation is entirely voluntary.

**Q: Who has operational control of flights?**
A: The aircraft operator (Part 135 certificate holder), not Sports Media or the broker.

**Q: What if there's an incident?**
A: Sports Media reports to League within 24 hours and provides full written report within 7 days.

### For League Compliance

**Q: What insurance coverage is in place?**
A: Multiple layers: operator ($25-50M), broker ($2M E&O + $5M non-owned), platform ($2M).

**Q: How is passenger data protected?**
A: Encrypted at rest and in transit, access controlled, not sold to third parties.

**Q: Can the League audit the program?**
A: Yes, with reasonable notice. Annual audits plus ad-hoc upon request.

### For League Security

**Q: How are operators vetted?**
A: FAA certificate verification, ARGUS/Wyvern safety rating, insurance review, enforcement action check.

**Q: What security measures are in place?**
A: TLS 1.3 encryption, SOC 2 roadmap, role-based access, comprehensive audit logging.

**Q: How are incidents handled?**
A: Immediate containment, 24-hour notification, full investigation, written report.
