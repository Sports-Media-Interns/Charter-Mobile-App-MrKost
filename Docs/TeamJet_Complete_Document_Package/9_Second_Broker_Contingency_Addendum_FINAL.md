# SECOND-BROKER CONTINGENCY ADDENDUM

## Schedule G to Master Services Agreement

**Sports Media, Inc.**

**Effective Date:** January 24, 2026

---

## ARTICLE 1: PURPOSE

### 1.1 Addendum Purpose

This Addendum establishes the conditions, procedures, and metrics under which Sports Media, Inc. ("Sports Media") may engage a secondary charter broker for Charter Requests while maintaining the Master Services Agreement with Chief Executive Air ("Primary Broker" or "Broker").

### 1.2 Intent

The parties agree that:

- Primary Broker is the exclusive preferred partner at launch
- This Addendum provides operational protection for Sports Media clients
- Triggering these provisions does NOT terminate the MSA
- Both parties are committed to avoiding trigger conditions
- The goal is successful partnership, not punitive measures

### 1.3 Relationship to MSA

This Addendum is incorporated into and subject to the Master Services Agreement. In case of conflict, this Addendum governs performance protection matters.

---

## ARTICLE 2: PERFORMANCE METRICS

### 2.1 Service Level Definitions

| Metric              | Definition                                         | Measurement            |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| **Response Time**   | Time from request receipt to Broker acknowledgment | Platform timestamp     |
| **Quote Delivery**  | Time from request receipt to first quote presented | Platform timestamp     |
| **Quote Quality**   | Quote meeting all required elements                | Checklist verification |
| **Availability**    | Broker ability to provide viable quote options     | Request outcome        |
| **Completion Rate** | Booked flights successfully completed              | Flight status          |

### 2.2 SLA Thresholds

| Request Type             | Response Target | Quote Target | Tolerance  |
| ------------------------ | --------------- | ------------ | ---------- |
| **Urgent** (<6 hrs)      | 10 minutes      | 30 minutes   | 0 minutes  |
| **Same-Day** (6-24 hrs)  | 15 minutes      | 45 minutes   | 5 minutes  |
| **Standard** (24-72 hrs) | 30 minutes      | 2 hours      | 15 minutes |
| **Advance** (>72 hrs)    | 1 hour          | 4 hours      | 30 minutes |

### 2.3 SLA Miss Definition

An "SLA Miss" occurs when:

- Response time exceeds target + tolerance, OR
- Quote delivery exceeds target + tolerance, OR
- Quote fails to meet quality standards, OR
- Broker is unable to respond at all

### 2.4 Excused Misses

The following do NOT count as SLA Misses:

- Platform system outage (documented)
- Force majeure events (natural disaster, etc.)
- Client-caused delays (incomplete information)
- Market-wide aircraft unavailability (documented)
- Requests outside normal scope (international requiring permits, etc.)

## ARTICLE 3: TRIGGER CONDITIONS

### 3.1 Tier 1 Trigger: Urgent SLA Pattern

**Condition:** Three (3) or more Urgent Request SLA Misses within any rolling thirty (30) day period.

**Effect:** Sports Media may route Urgent Requests to secondary broker.

**Scope:** Urgent requests only; other categories remain with Primary Broker.

### 3.2 Tier 2 Trigger: Cumulative SLA Pattern

**Condition:** Five (5) or more total SLA Misses (any category) within any rolling sixty (60) day period.

**Effect:** Sports Media may route all request categories to secondary broker at its discretion.

**Scope:** All request categories.

### 3.3 Tier 3 Trigger: Availability Pattern

**Condition:** Failure to provide any viable quote option for three (3) or more requests within any rolling thirty (30) day period where:

- Market availability reasonably exists (documented)
- Request is within normal program scope
- Client provided complete information

**Effect:** Sports Media may route requests in affected categories to secondary broker.

**Scope:** Categories where availability issues occurred.

### 3.4 Tier 4 Trigger: Service Quality Pattern

**Condition:** Two (2) or more documented client complaints regarding Broker service quality within any rolling sixty (60) day period where:

- Complaint is verified through client feedback
- Issue is attributable to Broker (not operator)
- Complaint is material (not minor inconvenience)

**Effect:** Sports Media may route affected client's requests to secondary broker.

**Scope:** Affected client account(s).

### 3.5 Immediate Trigger: Critical Failures

**Condition:** Any of the following:

- Arranging flight with non-certificated operator
- Providing falsified documentation
- Material breach of Part 295 requirements
- Safety incident attributable to Broker negligence

**Effect:** Immediate suspension of all request routing pending investigation.

**Scope:** All requests until resolved.

## ARTICLE 4: NOTIFICATION AND CURE

### 4.1 Performance Monitoring

Sports Media shall:

- Track all SLA metrics in real-time via platform
- Provide Broker access to performance dashboard
- Generate weekly SLA compliance reports
- Alert Broker when approaching trigger thresholds

### 4.2 Warning Notification

When Broker reaches 50% of any trigger threshold, Sports Media shall:

- Provide written warning notification
- Identify specific misses and dates
- Request written response within 48 hours
- Schedule performance review call

### 4.3 Trigger Notification

When a trigger condition is met, Sports Media shall:

- Provide written trigger notification within 24 hours
- Specify which trigger condition(s) are met
- Identify specific misses with documentation
- State intended actions (secondary broker routing)
- Initiate cure period

### 4.4 Cure Period

Upon trigger notification:

| Trigger Level | Cure Period            | Cure Requirement              |
| ------------- | ---------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Tier 1        | 10 business days       | Zero Urgent SLA misses        |
| Tier 2        | 15 business days       | Maximum 1 total SLA miss      |
| Tier 3        | 15 business days       | Viable quotes on all requests |
| Tier 4        | 15 business days       | Zero new complaints           |
| Immediate     | Investigation complete | Root cause addressed          |

### 4.5 Cure Demonstration

To demonstrate cure, Broker must:

- Achieve required performance during cure period
- Provide written corrective action plan
- Identify root causes addressed
- Implement preventive measures
- Confirm readiness to resume full operations

---

## ARTICLE 5: SECONDARY BROKER OPERATIONS

### 5.1 Secondary Broker Selection

Sports Media shall:

- Pre-qualify secondary broker(s) meeting same standards as Primary
- Maintain secondary broker agreement(s) on standby
- Not activate secondary until trigger conditions met
- Notify Primary Broker of secondary broker identity

### 5.2 Secondary Broker Requirements

Secondary broker must meet:

- All requirements in MSA Schedule D (Compliance)
- Equivalent SLA commitments
- Insurance requirements
- Safety rating requirements

### 5.3 Request Routing During Secondary Activation

When secondary broker is activated:

| Trigger Level | Primary Broker              | Secondary Broker           |
| ------------- | --------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Tier 1        | Standard, Same-Day, Advance | Urgent only                |
| Tier 2        | At Sports Media discretion  | At Sports Media discretion |
| Tier 3        | Unaffected categories       | Affected categories        |
| Tier 4        | Other clients               | Affected client(s)         |
| Immediate     | None (suspended)            | All requests               |

### 5.4 Right of First Refusal During Secondary

During secondary broker activation, Primary Broker retains right to:

- Receive notification of requests in activated categories
- Submit competing quotes (no SLA requirement)
- Win booking if quote is superior
- Demonstrate improved performance

---

## ARTICLE 6: RETURN TO EXCLUSIVITY

### 6.1 Return Conditions

Primary Broker may return to exclusive status upon:

| Trigger Level | Return Requirement                                     |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Tier 1        | 30 consecutive days with zero Urgent SLA misses        |
| Tier 2        | 30 consecutive days with maximum 2 total misses        |
| Tier 3        | 30 consecutive days with viable quotes on all requests |
| Tier 4        | 60 consecutive days with zero complaints               |
| Immediate     | Investigation cleared + corrective action verified     |

### 6.2 Return Process

1. Broker requests return to exclusivity in writing
2. Sports Media reviews performance data
3. Sports Media verifies corrective actions implemented
4. Parties conduct performance review meeting
5. Sports Media issues written return authorization
6. Secondary broker routing discontinued

### 6.3 Return Timeline

- Request review: 5 business days
- Meeting scheduling: 5 business days
- Authorization (if approved): 3 business days
- Implementation: Immediate upon authorization

### 6.4 Repeated Triggers

If the same trigger condition occurs twice within any twelve (12) month period:

- Cure period doubles
- Return requirement period doubles
- Sports Media may renegotiate exclusivity terms

---

## ARTICLE 7: EFFECT ON COMPENSATION

### 7.1 Compensation During Secondary Activation

- Primary Broker continues earning fees on bookings fulfilled
- Secondary broker fees are Primary Broker's responsibility (not Sports Media's)
- Sports Media retains all platform fees regardless of broker
- Quarterly bonuses calculated on all bookings (both brokers)

### 7.2 No Compensation Adjustment

Trigger activation does NOT:

- Reduce Primary Broker's fee rates
- Eliminate retainer recovery rights
- Affect payment terms
- Modify pricing structure

### 7.3 Volume Impact

If secondary broker activation significantly reduces Primary Broker volume:

- Quarterly bonus thresholds remain unchanged
- No minimum volume guarantees from Sports Media
- Primary Broker assumes volume risk during activation period

## ARTICLE 8: REPORTING AND TRANSPARENCY

### 8.1 Performance Dashboard

Sports Media shall provide Broker with:

- Real-time SLA compliance tracking
- Running totals toward trigger thresholds
- Historical performance trends
- Comparison to targets

### 8.2 Weekly Reports

Weekly reports shall include:

- Total requests by category
- SLA compliance rate by category
- Any misses with details
- Progress toward/away from triggers
- Client feedback summary

### 8.3 Monthly Reviews

Monthly performance reviews shall cover:

- Detailed SLA analysis
- Root cause analysis for any misses
- Corrective action status
- Trend analysis
- Upcoming challenges/opportunities

### 8.4 Dispute Resolution

If Broker disputes an SLA miss determination:

1. Broker submits dispute in writing within 5 business days
2. Sports Media reviews within 5 business days
3. If unresolved, escalate to Travel Director and Broker Owner
4. Resolution within 10 business days
5. Disputed miss excluded from count pending resolution

## ARTICLE 9: EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Tier 1 Trigger

**Scenario:**

- Day 1: Urgent request, quote delivered in 45 minutes (Miss #1)
- Day 12: Urgent request, quote delivered in 35 minutes (Miss #2)
- Day 25: Urgent request, quote delivered in 40 minutes (Miss #3)

**Result:**

- 3 Urgent misses within 30 days = Tier 1 triggered
- Sports Media may route Urgent requests to secondary broker
- 10 business day cure period begins
- Primary continues receiving Standard, Same-Day, Advance requests

### Example 2: Tier 3 Trigger

**Scenario:**

- Week 1: Request for NYC-MIA, no quote provided (Broker claims no availability)
- Week 2: Request for LAX-SFO, no quote provided (Broker claims no availability)
- Week 3: Request for CHI-DFW, no quote provided (Broker claims no availability)
- Sports Media documents that competitors sourced aircraft for similar routes

**Result:**

- 3 availability failures with market availability = Tier 3 triggered
- Sports Media may route domestic short-haul to secondary
- Broker must demonstrate sourcing capability improvement

### Example 3: Successful Cure

**Scenario:**

- Tier 2 triggered on March 1 (5 total misses in 60 days)
- Cure period: March 1-21 (15 business days)
- During cure: Broker achieves 1 total miss
- Broker submits corrective action plan
- Broker requests return to exclusivity April 1
- April 1-30: Zero misses

**Result:**

- Cure requirement met (max 1 miss during cure)
- 30-day return requirement met (zero misses in April)
- Full exclusivity restored May 1

### 10.1 Good Faith

Both parties agree to:

- Exercise good faith in performance measurement
- Not manipulate metrics or timing
- Communicate proactively about issues
- Work collaboratively toward success

### 10.2 Not Punitive

This Addendum is designed for:

- Client protection (not punishment)

- Performance improvement (not relationship termination)

### 10.3 Partnership Preservation

Trigger activation:

- Does NOT indicate relationship failure
- Does NOT affect non-performance aspects of partnership
- Does NOT prevent return to full exclusivity
- Does NOT reduce good faith obligations
