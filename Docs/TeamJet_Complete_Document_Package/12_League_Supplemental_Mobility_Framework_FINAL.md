## RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, League governs professional sports competition among its member clubs and establishes standards for approved vendors and service providers;

**WHEREAS**, Platform Provider operates a sports-specific technology platform for routing charter aviation requests to properly certificated third-party air carriers and charter brokers;

**WHEREAS**, League desires to make available to its member clubs a voluntary, supplemental program for short-notice charter mobility needs that does not replace existing team travel arrangements;

**WHEREAS**, the Parties wish to establish clear regulatory positioning, roles, and responsibilities to protect League, member clubs, and Platform Provider;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## ARTICLE 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Program Purpose

The purpose of this Framework Agreement is to establish a league-approved, **non-exclusive, optional** program under which member clubs may elect to use the Platform Provider's sports mobility request platform (the "Platform") to route and manage short-notice charter aviation requests.

### 1.2 Covered Use Cases

The Platform is designed for the following use cases:

| Category           | Examples                                                  |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Executive Travel   | C-suite, ownership, board members, front office           |
| Player Movement    | Non-team travel: trades, transactions, physicals, rehab   |
| Family Mobility    | Approved player and executive family travel               |
| Sponsor/VIP Travel | Partner hospitality, investor roadshows, brand activation |
| Event Travel       | League meetings, draft, combines, appearances             |
| Emergency          | Medical, security, crisis situations                      |

### 1.3 Exclusions

This Framework Agreement **does NOT cover**:

- Full team charter operations (roster travel)
- League-mandated team transportation
- Travel governed by collective bargaining agreements
- Air ambulance or medical evacuation services
- Any travel requiring Part 121 or scheduled service

### 1.4 Supplemental Nature

**This program is supplemental and non-exclusive.** It is intended to address edge cases and short-notice needs that are not efficiently handled by primary team travel arrangements. Nothing in this Framework Agreement is intended to replace or interfere with:

- Existing team charter contracts
- League travel programs
- Club travel practices for team transport
- Relationships with existing airline or charter providers

## ARTICLE 2: REGULATORY POSITIONING AND COMPLIANCE

### 2.1 Platform Provider Status

**Platform Provider is NOT an air carrier and does NOT provide air transportation.**

Platform Provider's role is limited to:

| Function           | Description                                                    |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Technology         | Providing the software platform for request intake and routing |
| Coordination       | Routing requests to Fulfillment Partners                       |
| Workflow           | Managing authorization and approval workflows                  |
| Compliance Logging | Maintaining audit trails and SLA monitoring                    |
| Reporting          | Generating usage and performance analytics                     |

### 2.2 What Platform Provider Does NOT Do

Platform Provider shall NOT:

- Operate aircraft or provide air transportation
- Select the air carrier for any flight
- Control aircraft operations or flight dispatch
- Hold out to the public as a charter broker or operator
- Set or control air transportation prices
- Execute charter contracts for carriage
- Provide safety or operational guidance to air carriers
- Make representations about flight safety beyond operator-provided data

### 2.3 Fulfillment Partner Role

"Fulfillment Partners" are properly licensed charter brokers and/or certificated air carriers responsible for:

| Responsibility        | Description                                 |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Aircraft Sourcing     | Identifying and securing available aircraft |
| Charter Contracting   | Executing contracts for air transportation  |
| Operator Selection    | Choosing certificated operators             |
| Operational Execution | Coordinating flights and crews              |
| FAA/DOT Compliance    | Maintaining required certifications         |
| Safety Compliance     | Ensuring regulatory safety standards        |
| Insurance             | Maintaining aviation liability coverage     |

### 2.4 Contracts for Carriage

**All air transportation shall be provided under contracts between:**

- The traveler or club (as applicable), AND
- The certificated air carrier and/or properly licensed broker

These contracts are **separate from** this Framework Agreement and Platform Provider is not a party to them.

### 2.5 Regulatory Standards

| Requirement            | Standard                                                   |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Operator Certification | FAA Part 135 (or other appropriate authority)              |
| Broker Licensing       | DOT Part 295 (where applicable)                            |
| Insurance              | Aviation liability customary for charter operations        |
| Safety Audits          | ARGUS Gold/Platinum or Wyvern Registered/Wingman preferred |

## ARTICLE 3: NON-EXCLUSIVE, OPT-IN STRUCTURE

### 3.1 Non-Exclusive Program

League acknowledges and agrees that this program is:

- **Supplemental** to existing travel arrangements
- **Non-exclusive** - clubs may use other providers
- **Voluntary** - no club is required to participate
- **Non-mandatory** - League is not mandating use

### 3.2 Club Opt-In Process

Each club that wishes to participate shall:

1. Execute a separate **Club Addendum** with Platform Provider
2. Designate authorized users and roles
3. Configure approval workflows per club policy
4. Accept applicable platform fees (if any)

### 3.3 Club Opt-Out

Clubs may discontinue use at any time subject to:

- Notice requirements in their Club Addendum
- Settlement of outstanding fees
- Completion of in-progress requests (at club's option)

### 3.4 No Replacement of Team Travel

**Nothing in this Framework Agreement replaces or interferes with:**

- Existing team travel arrangements
- League travel programs or policies
- Collective bargaining obligations
- Club charter practices for roster travel

## ARTICLE 5: LEAGUE APPROVALS AND BRAND USE

### 5.1 No Implied League Endorsement

Unless expressly approved in writing by League:

- Platform Provider may NOT state or imply League endorses the Platform
- Platform Provider may NOT state or imply League endorses any Fulfillment Partner
- No "Official" designation without written approval
- No League marks usage without written approval

Platform Provider may state:

- "League Approved Vendor for supplemental charter mobility services"
- "Available to [League] member clubs on an opt-in basis"

Platform Provider may NOT state:

- "Official charter provider of [League]"
- "[League] requires use of Sports Media Charter"
- "[League] endorses [Fulfillment Partner]"

### 5.3 Marks and Naming

| Requirement       | Standard                            |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| League Marks      | Pre-approval required in writing    |
| Club Marks        | Club approval required              |
| "Official" Status | Separate written agreement required |
| Co-Branding       | Marketing addendum required         |

### 5.4 Sponsor Activation

If League permits sponsor activation related to the program:

- Such activation shall be documented in a separate marketing addendum
- Activation shall not alter the Parties' compliance positioning in Article 2
- Platform Provider remains a technology provider, not a sponsor

## ARTICLE 7: DATA, PRIVACY, AND CONFIDENTIALITY

### 7.1 Data Ownership

| Data Type                     | Owner             |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------- |
| Platform Software             | Platform Provider |
| Platform Configuration        | Platform Provider |
| Analytics Methods             | Platform Provider |
| Club Confidential Information | Club              |
| Traveler Data                 | Club              |

Platform Provider has limited rights to process club and traveler data solely for service delivery.

### 7.2 Permitted Data Use

Platform Provider may use **de-identified, aggregated data** to:

- Improve the Platform
- Produce benchmarking reports
- Analyze industry trends

**Provided** no club or individual is identifiable without written consent.

### 7.3 Prohibited Data Use

Platform Provider shall NOT:

- Sell club or traveler data
- Share data with third parties except Fulfillment Partners (for service delivery)
- Use data for marketing without consent
- Retain data beyond retention period

### 7.4 Confidentiality

| Obligation           | Standard                                         |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| General              | Reasonable care, industry standards              |
| Club Information     | Professional sports business operations standard |
| Traveler Information | Privacy law compliance                           |
| Duration             | Survives termination for 5 years                 |

### 7.5 Security Standards

Platform Provider shall maintain administrative, technical, and physical safeguards including:

| Control        | Standard                             |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Encryption     | TLS 1.3 in transit, AES-256 at rest  |
| Access Control | Role-based, least privilege          |
| Authentication | MFA for privileged access            |
| Monitoring     | Logging, alerting, incident response |
| Compliance     | SOC 2 roadmap, CCPA compliant        |

## ARTICLE 8: COMMERCIAL TERMS

### 8.1 Club Commercials

Commercial terms for club participation, including:

- Platform fees
- Retainers
- Service fees
- Subscription tiers

Shall be set forth in each **Club Addendum** and are not governed by this Framework Agreement.

### 8.2 Fulfillment Pricing

- Pricing for charter flights is set by Fulfillment Partners and/or certificated air carriers
- Platform Provider does NOT set air transportation prices
- Platform Provider may present quotes received from Fulfillment Partners
- Platform Provider does NOT control pricing decisions

### 8.3 League Administration Fee

If League requires a league administration fee:

- It shall be specified in an attachment to this Framework Agreement
- It shall NOT be contingent on flight completion
- It may be structured as annual fee or per-club fee

### 8.4 No Hidden Fees

All fees shall be:

- Disclosed in writing before execution
- Documented in applicable agreements
- Transparent and itemized

## ARTICLE 9: RISK ALLOCATION, INSURANCE, AND INDEMNIFICATION

### 9.1 Operational Liability

**Operational flight risk, safety obligations, and aviation regulatory compliance reside solely with:**

- The certificated air carrier, AND
- Where applicable, the charter broker

Platform Provider assumes NO operational liability for:

- Flight operations
- Aircraft maintenance
- Crew qualifications
- Weather decisions
- Delays, cancellations, or diversions
- Passenger injuries or property damage during flights

### 9.2 Insurance Requirements

| Party                | Coverage                     | Minimum                  |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Platform Provider    | Commercial General Liability | $2,000,000               |
| Platform Provider    | Cyber Liability              | $2,000,000               |
| Platform Provider    | Professional Liability (E&O) | $2,000,000               |
| Fulfillment Partners | Errors & Omissions           | $2,000,000               |
| Fulfillment Partners | Non-Owned Aircraft           | $5,000,000               |
| Air Carriers         | Aviation Liability           | Customary for operations |

### 9.3 Certificate Requirements

- Certificates provided upon request
- League named as certificate holder (upon request)
- 30 days notice of cancellation or material change

### 9.4 Indemnification

Each Party shall indemnify the other for third-party claims arising from:

- Its own negligence
- Willful misconduct
- Material breach of this Framework Agreement

Subject to limitations set forth in Club Addenda and Fulfillment Partner Agreements.

### 9.5 Limitation of Liability

| Type                   | Limitation                                 |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Direct Damages         | Fees paid in prior 12 months               |
| Indirect/Consequential | Excluded                                   |
| Exceptions             | Fraud, willful misconduct, indemnification |

### 9.6 No Warranty of Availability

**Platform Provider does NOT warrant:**

- Aircraft availability
- Specific pricing
- Acceptance by any air carrier
- Specific aircraft types
- Flight completion

## ARTICLE 10: TERM, TERMINATION, AND TRANSITION

### 10.1 Initial Term

The initial term is **one (1) year** from the Effective Date.

### 10.2 Renewal

This Framework Agreement renews automatically for additional **one (1) year** terms unless either Party gives **thirty (30) days** written notice of non-renewal.

### 10.3 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Framework Agreement upon **thirty (30) days** written notice.

### 10.4 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate immediately for material breach that remains uncured after **ten (10) days** written notice, including:

- Failure to maintain required insurance
- Violation of regulatory positioning requirements
- Breach of confidentiality obligations
- Unauthorized use of marks

### 10.5 Effect on Clubs

Upon termination of this Framework Agreement:

- Existing Club Addenda may continue at club's option
- No new Club Addenda may be executed
- Platform Provider continues serving clubs for addenda duration
- League approval/vendor status terminates

### 10.6 Transition

Upon termination, Platform Provider shall provide:

- Reasonable transition plan for active club accounts
- Data export within 30 days
- Cooperation with successor provider (if any)
- Compliance with security and confidentiality requirements

The Parties will attempt to resolve disputes through executive escalation:

| Level           | Timeline      | Participants        |
| --------------- | ------------- | ------------------- |
| Working Level   | 10 days       | Operations contacts |
| Executive Level | 20 days       | Senior executives   |
| Formal Process  | After 30 days | Per Section 11.3    |

### 11.2 Governing Law

This Framework Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the **State of Colorado**, without regard to conflict-of-law principles, unless League requires a different jurisdiction stated in an attachment.

### 11.3 Dispute Resolution

If executive escalation fails:

- **Mediation** in the governing jurisdiction
- If mediation fails, **binding arbitration** under AAA Commercial Rules
- Venue in the agreed jurisdiction

### 11.4 Continued Performance

During any dispute, Parties shall continue performing their obligations to the extent reasonably possible.
