# AGENCY OF RECORD & SPONSORSHIP ADDENDUM

## Schedule F to Master Services Agreement

**Sports Media, Inc.**

**Effective Date:** January 24, 2026

---

## ARTICLE 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose

This Addendum establishes Sports Media, Inc. as the exclusive Agency of Record ("AOR") for advertising, marketing, and sponsorship opportunities that arise through relationships developed under the Master Services Agreement between Sports Media and Chief Executive Air.

### 1.2 Strategic Rationale

The Sports Media Charter program creates unique access to:

- Professional sports team decision-makers
- Team ownership and executive leadership
- Sponsor and corporate partner networks
- League operations and marketing personnel
- High-net-worth individuals in the sports ecosystem

This access generates opportunities for advertising, sponsorship, and marketing services beyond charter procurement.

### 1.3 AOR Scope

Sports Media shall serve as exclusive AOR for opportunities including:

| Category                       | Services                                              |
| ------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| **Digital Advertising**        | Display, video, social media, programmatic, CTV/OTT   |
| **Out-of-Home (OOH)**          | Billboards, transit, airport advertising              |
| **Digital Out-of-Home (DOOH)** | Digital billboards, venue screens, LED signage        |
| **In-Venue Advertising**       | Arena/stadium signage, concourse displays, LED boards |
| **Sponsorship Services**       | Strategy, negotiation, activation, measurement        |
| **Media Planning & Buying**    | Traditional and digital media placement               |
| **Creative Services**          | Campaign development, production, design              |
| **Influencer Marketing**       | Athlete partnerships, NIL deals, ambassador programs  |
| **Experiential Marketing**     | Events, activations, hospitality experiences          |
| **Content Production**         | Video, photography, branded content                   |

## ARTICLE 2: QUALIFYING OPPORTUNITIES

### 2.1 Covered Opportunities

AOR designation applies to opportunities that:

1. **Originate from Program Relationships**
   - Contacts introduced through Sports Media Charter platform
   - Relationships developed from charter bookings
   - Introductions made by Sports Media to teams/sponsors
   - Connections through League Framework Agreements

2. **Relate to Sports/Travel Ecosystem**
   - Team marketing and advertising
   - Sponsor acquisition and activation
   - Travel industry partnerships
   - Hospitality and VIP experiences

3. **Involve Broker Introductions**
   - Clients introduced by Chief Executive Air
   - Broker relationship-driven opportunities
   - Joint sales efforts with Broker participation

### 2.2 Excluded Opportunities

AOR designation does NOT apply to:

- Pre-existing agency relationships documented before Effective Date
- Opportunities unrelated to sports, travel, or hospitality
- Government contracts requiring specific procurement processes
- Opportunities explicitly excluded by mutual written agreement

### 2.3 Documentation Requirements

For AOR rights to apply, Sports Media must:

- Document the originating relationship in writing
- Notify Broker within 30 days of identifying opportunity
- Provide reasonable evidence of program-related origin

---

## ARTICLE 3: RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL

### 3.1 Notification Requirement

When an advertising, sponsorship, or marketing opportunity is identified:

1. The identifying party notifies Sports Media within 10 business days
2. Sports Media has 30 days to submit a proposal
3. Client/prospect must allow Sports Media to present proposal
4. Client may not engage another agency until Sports Media declines or fails to respond

### 3.2 Proposal Requirements

Sports Media proposals shall include:

- Scope of services
- Deliverables and timeline
- Pricing and fee structure
- Team/personnel assignments
- Relevant case studies or credentials

### 3.3 Client Decision

After receiving Sports Media's proposal:

- Client has 15 days to respond
- Client may accept, reject, or negotiate
- If rejected, client may pursue other agencies
- Sports Media receives notification of rejection reason

### 4.1 Media Commission

| Media Type                             | Commission Rate       |
| -------------------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Digital Media (Display, Video, Social) | 10-15% of media spend |
| Traditional Media (TV, Radio, Print)   | 12-15% of media spend |
| Out-of-Home / DOOH                     | 10-12% of media spend |
| Programmatic / CTV                     | 12-15% of media spend |

### 4.2 Production Markup

| Production Type      | Markup Rate               |
| -------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Video Production     | 15-20% of production cost |
| Creative Development | 15-20% of creative fees   |
| Event Production     | 12-15% of event cost      |
| Content Creation     | 15-20% of content cost    |

### 4.3 Sponsorship Fees

| Sponsorship Service     | Fee Structure               |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Sponsorship Strategy    | 5-10% of sponsorship value  |
| Sponsorship Negotiation | 10-15% of sponsorship value |
| Sponsorship Activation  | 15-20% of activation budget |
| Sponsorship Measurement | $5,000-$25,000 per program  |

### 4.4 Retainer Arrangements

For ongoing relationships, parties may establish:

- Monthly retainers for dedicated resources
- Annual contracts with committed scope
- Hybrid models (retainer + performance fees)

### 4.5 Fee Negotiation

- Rates above are guidelines; actual fees negotiated per engagement
- Volume discounts available for significant commitments
- Performance bonuses may apply for measurable results

- Named Account Director for each major client

### 5.2 Quality Standards

Sports Media commits to:

- Industry-standard creative quality

- Transparent reporting and analytics

Sports Media may engage subcontractors for:

### 6.1 Client Ownership

Client owns:

- All creative work product developed for client
- Campaign assets, designs, and content
- Data and analytics generated for client
- Trademark and brand materials

### 6.2 Sports Media Ownership

Sports Media retains:

- Proprietary processes and methodologies
- Pre-existing tools and templates
- Aggregate, anonymized industry data

- Client grants Sports Media license to use client marks for campaign execution
- Sports Media grants client perpetual license to work product upon payment

## ARTICLE 8: CONFIDENTIALITY

### 8.1 Confidential Information

AOR engagements involve access to:

- Marketing strategies and budgets
- Competitive intelligence
- Customer/fan data
- Sponsorship terms and valuations
- Upcoming campaigns and announcements

### 8.2 Protection Requirements

Sports Media shall:

- Limit access to need-to-know personnel
- Not share information with competitors
- Not use information for other clients without consent
- Maintain confidentiality for 5 years post-engagement

### 8.3 Conflict Management

If Sports Media serves multiple sports clients:

- Dedicated teams assigned per client
- Information barriers maintained
- Conflicts disclosed and managed
- Client approval required for competitive category work

## ARTICLE 9: TERM AND TERMINATION

### 9.1 AOR Term

AOR designation runs concurrent with Master Services Agreement:

- Initial Term: Five (5) years
- Renewal: Per MSA renewal terms

### 9.2 Individual Engagement Terms

Specific campaigns/projects have individual terms as agreed in statements of work.

### 9.3 Termination of AOR Status

AOR status terminates if:

- Master Services Agreement terminates
- Sports Media materially breaches AOR obligations
- Mutual written agreement to terminate

### 9.4 Transition Assistance

Upon termination:

- Sports Media provides transition support for 90 days
- All work product and assets delivered to client
- Campaign continuity maintained during transition
- Final billing and reconciliation completed

## ARTICLE 11: EXAMPLES AND SCENARIOS

### Scenario 1: Team Sponsorship Opportunity

**Situation:** Through Sports Media Charter, Sports Media develops relationship with NBA team. Team seeks presenting sponsor for new training facility.

**AOR Application:**

- Sports Media presents sponsorship strategy proposal
- If accepted, Sports Media negotiates with potential sponsors
- Sports Media earns 10-15% of sponsorship value secured
- Sports Media may also provide activation services

### Scenario 2: Sponsor Advertising Campaign

**Situation:** Corporate sponsor uses Sports Media Charter for VIP travel. Sponsor seeks advertising campaign targeting sports fans.

**AOR Application:**

- Sports Media presents media planning proposal
- If accepted, Sports Media plans and buys media
- Sports Media earns 10-15% media commission
- May include creative development (additional fees)

### Scenario 3: League-Wide Partnership

**Situation:** League Framework Agreement leads to league marketing relationship. League seeks agency for fan engagement campaign.

**AOR Application:**

- Sports Media has right of first refusal
- 30 days to present comprehensive proposal
- If awarded, Sports Media serves as lead agency
- Full agency fees apply per scope
